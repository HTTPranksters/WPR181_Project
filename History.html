History of the World Wide Web

  • Back to main page

The history of the internet dates all the way back to the 1960s, which is when the Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA) of the U.S Department of Defence started the development of the ARPANET. This network was created to connect various research institutions. These are some of the important milestones and when they were achieved:

1960-1969

  • 1962: J.C.R. Licklider introduced the concept of a “Galactic Network”, which was a vision of creating a globally connected set of computers.
  • 1965: Lawrence G. Roberts wrote down the first plan for the ARPANET, a network that would have allowed computers to be able to share their resources with each other.
  • 1969: The ARPANET was established, which connected the University of Los Angeles, the Stanford research institute, the university of Santa Barbra, and the university of California, which was the birth of the internet. On the 29th of October the first message was sent, which was between the University of Los Angeles (UCLA) and the Stanford Research Institute (SRI).

1970-1979

  • 1971: Ray Tomlinson sent the first email and selected the “@” symbol for email addresses.
  • 1973: Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn wrote a paper, which described the Transmission Control Protocol (TCP), a very important communication protocol.
  • 1974: Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn published the specifications for the TCP protocol.

1980-1989

  • 1981: Ira Fuchs and Greydon Freeman founded the BITNET (Because It's Time Network), which was used to connect universities.
  • 1982: The term “internet” was used for the first time.
  • 1983: On the 1st of January TCP/IP was adopted as the standard for the ARPANET, which standardized internet communication. Domain Name System (DNS) was introduced, which simplified addresses on the internet.
  • 1985: The very first “.com” domain was registered.
  • 1986: National Science Foundation (NSF) funded supercomputer centres and created the NSFNET Project, which provided access to these supercomputer centres.
  • 1989: Tim Berners-Lee proposed the idea of the World Wide Web (WWW).

1990-1999

  • 1990: Tim Berners-Lee created the world’s first web browser and server.
  • 1991: The World Wide Web was made publicly accessible
  • 1993: National Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA) released Mosaic 1.0, a web browser that gave access to text and graphics.
  • 1994: The commercialisation of the internet started which consisted of the creation and management of online services for financial gain among other things.
  • 1995: Decommissioning of NSFNET took place. By the mid-1990s the growth of the internet and the number of users had passed the capabilities of the NSFNET. They then made the decision to phase out of running the backbone network.
  • 1998: Larry Page and Sergey Brin, students at Stanford University founded Google.

2000-Present

  • 2000s: The popularity of social media platforms like YouTube, Instagram and Twitter rose, which changed the way people communicated and shared information.
  • 2010s: App stores were introduced, which created the app economy for mobile devices. Cloud computing and the Internet of Things (IoT) were introduced.
  • 2020s: 5G technology was introduced and advancements in artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning, blockchain, and quantum computing was made.
